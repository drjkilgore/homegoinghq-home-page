HOMEGOINGHQ — COOKIE POLICY

Last Updated: 7/18/2026 | Version: 1.0

1. WHAT COOKIES ARE

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. Similar technologies include pixels, local storage, and SDKs. We refer to all of these as “cookies.” This Policy explains how HomegoingHQ uses them and how you can control them. It supplements our Privacy Policy.

2. WHY WE USE COOKIES

We use cookies to operate the Platform, keep you signed in, remember your preferences, secure the Platform, understand usage, and (where permitted) measure and improve performance.

3. CATEGORIES OF COOKIES WE USE

Category Purpose Consent required? Examples / Providers
Strictly necessary Authentication, security, load balancing, core functionality (e.g., keeping you logged in, remembering your cart/plan selection) No (always active) Session/auth tokens; Supabase auth; Netlify
Functional Remember preferences and settings (e.g., brand/theme, dismissed prompts) Yes, where required Local storage keys; Netlify
Analytics/Performance Understand usage to improve the Platform Yes google analytics —
Advertising/Targeting Measure or deliver marketing (not used) Yes

4. HOW LONG COOKIES LAST

Cookies are either session cookies (deleted when you close your browser) or persistent cookies (remain for a set period or until deleted). Durations for each cookie are listed in our consent tool.

5. YOUR CHOICES

  • Consent tool: Use our on-site cookie preferences tool to accept or reject non-essential cookies and to change your choices at any time.
  • Browser controls: Most browsers let you block or delete cookies; blocking strictly necessary cookies may impair the Platform.
  • Opt-out signals: We honor recognized preference signals (including Global Privacy Control) where required by law.

6. CHANGES

We may update this Policy; the “Last Updated” date reflects the latest version.
